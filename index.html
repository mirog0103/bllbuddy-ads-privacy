B$LL Buddy – Ads & Data Privacy Policy

Effective: 30 Aug 2025 • Contact: mirog0103@gmail.com

This page explains how B$LL Buddy (“we”, “us”) uses advertising technologies (Google AdMob and selected mediation partners) in the iOS app. It supplements our main Privacy Policy and focuses on ad-related data.

What we collect for ads

  • Ad performance and interaction (e.g., impressions, taps, frequency capping).
  • Approximate location (city/region) inferred by the ad provider (we do not collect GPS).
  • Device and app info (app version, OS version, model, language, IP for fraud-prevention).
  • Identifiers used for ads (e.g., IDFA if you consent / ATT allows it; otherwise limited or none).

We do not access your Contacts, Camera/Photos, or precise (GPS) location.

Who processes ad data

We serve ads via Google AdMob and may use mediation to request ads from multiple ad networks. Each partner acts under its own privacy policy:

  • Google AdMob / Google Marketing Platform – policies.google.com/privacy
  • AppLovin (MAX) – applovin.com/privacy/
  • Unity – unity.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • ironSource (LevelPlay) – is.com/privacy-policy/

Legal basis & region-specific info

In the EEA/UK, we rely on your consent (GDPR/UK GDPR) obtained through Google’s User Messaging Platform (UMP). If you decline consent, we show contextual or limited ads when available. In California, we honor “Do Not Sell or Share” choices under CPRA via AdMob’s privacy controls.

Your choices

  • Consent (EEA/UK): You’ll see a consent form on first launch and when required by Google.
  • Change your choice: In the app, go to Settings → Privacy → Manage Ads Consent (when available). You can also adjust iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.
  • Limit Ad Tracking: iOS → Settings → Privacy & Security → Apple Advertising / Tracking.

Data retention

Ad vendors retain data per their policies for reporting, fraud prevention, and compliance. We do not store raw ad identifiers in our databases.

Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 (or the local age of digital consent). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

Updates

We may update this page as partners or settings change. The effective date will be updated accordingly.
